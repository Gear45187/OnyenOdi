ACSS Class of 94: A Journey of Growth and Excellence

The Association of Classical Secondary School (ACSS) Class of 94 has come a long way since its inception. In this update, we'll take a walk down memory lane, highlighting the key milestones and achievements of our association.



Humble Beginnings (2021)

The ACSS Class of 94 officially took off in 2021 under the visionary leadership of Hon. Wale Martins. During his tenure, the association made significant strides, including:


Registration with CAC: The association was formally registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC), marking a significant milestone in its journey.



Our CAC Registration certicate


Constitution Drafting and Harmonization: A constitution was drafted and harmonized, providing a framework for the association's governance and operations

First Reunion Party (2022): The association organized its first reunion party in 2022, bringing together classmates and rekindling old friendships after 28years of leaving secondary school.





Expansion and Growth (2023-2024)



As the association continued to grow, it organized two successful end-of-year parties:


End of Year Party (2023): The association celebrated the end of 2023 with a festive party, fostering camaraderie and a sense of community among its members.




End of Year Party/Handing Over Ceremony (2024): The association marked the end of 2024 with a grand party, an alleviation programme for her students in the alma mater giving out 2pairs of uniforms and and a pair of sandals to 24 Vulnerable students as a way of thanking God for His blessings and bringing us this far in life, which also served as a handing over ceremony for the outgoing President, Hon. Wale Martins. Pictures of the event here too.

A New Era of Excellence (2024-Present)
Under Madam Stella's leadership, the ACSS Class of 94 has continued to soar to new heights. Some of the key initiatives and achievements include:
30th Anniversary Celebrations and Handover Ceremony: The association celebrated its 30th anniversary, coinciding with the handover ceremony from Hon. Wale Martins to Madam Stella Onyendinazu.



Sponsorship of JAMB Forms

The association has sponsored 28 JAMB forms for deserving students in its alma mater.





School Sick Bay


The alumni sick Bay project was initiated to provide much needed healthcare facility for our alma matter.this project aimed to enhance the well being and health of student. The class of '94 sick bay project involved renovation and equipment provision.through class effort it was successfully completed, ensuring a safe and equipped space for care. The alumni project stands as a testament to the power of collaboration and community spirit. We hope this facility will serve the school community for years to come.
